05 / System Design Thinking

How I think about backend systems.

Architecture Principles
  • Prefer event-driven architectures when throughput, decoupling, and async fault isolation matter.
  • Use idempotency, retries, and dead-letter strategies to keep distributed workflows correct under failure.
  • Optimize latency through caching, pool tuning, batching, and removing unnecessary remote round-trips.
  • Design APIs and services to scale horizontally before vertical scaling becomes necessary.
Operational Thinking
  • Balance consistency vs availability based on the domain: stricter for billing and payment, looser for analytics.
  • Use circuit breakers, backoff, and graceful degradation for downstream dependency failures.
  • Handle concurrency explicitly with distributed locking or atomic operations when duplicate side effects are expensive.
  • Build systems with observability in mind so performance bottlenecks and production regressions are measurable.
